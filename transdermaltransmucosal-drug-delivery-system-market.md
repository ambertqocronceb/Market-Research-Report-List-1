<p><p>The Transdermal and Transmucosal Drug Delivery System market encompasses two primary types of drug delivery methods. Transdermal Drug Delivery Systems involve administering drugs through the skin via patches or gels, ensuring controlled, sustained release into the bloodstream. In contrast, Transmucosal Drug Delivery Systems deliver medications through mucosal surfaces, such as the oral cavity or nasal passages, allowing for rapid absorption into circulation. Both systems aim to enhance therapeutic efficacy, improve patient compliance, and minimize side effects associated with traditional administration methods.</p></p>
